Labour rights

Non-payment of compensation for additional leave: constitutional legal assessment

Kenan had worked for many years at the same enterprise. He approached his work responsibly and worked tirelessly for the well-being of his family. In accordance with the legislation and the conditions of his work, he had also acquired the right to additional leave.

But life changed unexpectedly.

As a result of staff reductions at the enterprise, Kenan‚Äôs employment contract was terminated. When leaving his job, he expected to receive compensation for the leave days he had not used. Although payment was made for his basic leave, no compensation was provided for his additional leave.

For Kenan, this was not merely a legal issue. It meant that the value of his work was not fully recognized.

He applied to the court to restore his rights. However, since the existing legislation did not provide compensation for additional leave, his claim was not satisfied.

This situation made Kenan ask an important question: some workers in similar circumstances received compensation, while others did not. Was this fair?

The matter was examined by the Commissioner for Human Rights (Ombudsman) of the Republic of Azerbaijan. As a result of the legal analysis, it was determined that this restriction raised concerns from the perspective of human rights, especially the principle of equality.

On the basis of the Ombudsman‚Äôs request, the matter was submitted to the Constitutional Court.

In its decision, the Constitutional Court stated that the non-payment of compensation for additional leave disproportionately restricted the employee‚Äôs rights and was not in line with the constitutional principle of equality.

As a result, the application of this restriction was suspended and the legislation was required to be brought into conformity with the requirements of the Constitution.

Social rights

Salim‚Äôs story

Salim is a father raising five children on his own. Life did not place difficult choices before him ‚ÄĒ it placed responsibilities. He is both the father and the only support carrying the full burden of the family.

Due to financial difficulties, Salim applies for social assistance. However, under the legislation in force, this assistance applied only to mothers with more than five children. Although he was in a similar situation, he could not benefit from this right.

The matter was examined by the Commissioner for Human Rights of the Republic of Azerbaijan and an application was submitted to the Constitutional Court.

The Constitutional Court found that the existing norm was not contrary to the Constitution. At the same time, it noted that there was a gap in legal regulation.

Therefore, it was recommended that the legislation be improved in order to also take into account fathers raising children alone.

This story shows that sometimes the law is not violated, but the realities of life require legal approaches to be reconsidered.
