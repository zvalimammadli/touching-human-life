Attendance Requirement and Ensuring the Right to Education

Sanan was studying at a higher education institution. During the semester, due to a serious illness, he was unable to attend classes for a long period. Nevertheless, he did not completely fall behind in the educational process, studied the subjects independently and prepared for the exams.

At the end of the semester, however, the university informed him that under the existing rules he could not be admitted to the exams because he had not attended classes sufficiently. The rules required a student to attend a certain portion of the course load, and exceeding that threshold ‚ÄĒ that is, missing more than 25 percent of class hours ‚ÄĒ was considered a ground for non-admission to the exams. This norm was applied without exception, and Sanan‚Äôs illness was not taken into account.

Thus, the issue was whether the application of such an attendance rule without considering valid reasons could lead to a restriction of the student‚Äôs right to education. The Commissioner for Human Rights (Ombudsman) of the Republic of Azerbaijan applied to the Constitutional Court for a legal assessment of this matter.

When examining the case, the Constitutional Court noted that the right to education is one of the fundamental rights guaranteed by the Constitution, and that the State has the authority to establish certain rules in this field. However, when applying those rules, fairness and proportionality must be ensured. Restricting a right should not eliminate its essence.

The Court particularly emphasized that the automatic application of the attendance requirement without considering valid reasons, including health conditions, could lead to a disproportionate restriction of the student‚Äôs right to education. Therefore, individual circumstances should be assessed in such cases, and the rules should be applied in that context.

As a result, this approach demonstrated that legal norms should be applied not only formally, but also in accordance with the essence of human rights. In ensuring the right to education, the concrete situation of each individual must be taken into account.
